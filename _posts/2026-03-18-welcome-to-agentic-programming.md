This blog explores the emerging field of agentic programming — building software with AI coding agents as active collaborators rather than passive tools.

## What We'll Cover

- **Architecture decisions** — how to structure projects so AI agents can work with them effectively
- **Tool choices** — which frameworks, ORMs, and libraries play well with agentic workflows
- **Patterns and anti-patterns** — what works and what doesn't when AI is writing your code
- **Real-world experience** — lessons learned from building production software with AI agents

## Why This Matters

The way we write software is changing. AI agents can now scaffold projects, write features, fix bugs, and refactor code. But not all codebases are equally amenable to this. The choices you make early — your stack, your abstractions, your testing strategy — determine how effectively an agent can contribute.

This blog is a running journal of those choices and their consequences.

Stay tuned.
